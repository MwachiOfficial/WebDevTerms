Extranet

An Extranet is a controlled extension of an intranet. It allows authorized external users—such as partners, vendors, suppliers, or key customers—to access specific parts of a company's internal network.

Role in Technology: It acts as a "bridge" for B2B (Business-to-Business) collaboration. For example, a car manufacturer might use an extranet to allow parts suppliers to check inventory levels in real-time.
Security: It requires robust authentication (like Multi-Factor Authentication) to ensure external users only see what they are permitted to see, keeping sensitive internal data private.
